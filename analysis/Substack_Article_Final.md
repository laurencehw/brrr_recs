## Six Quick Wins for 2025

If I had to pick six reforms that could happen this year:

### 1. Grid Connection Fast-Lane (0-6 months)
**What:** 120-day clock for renewable connections; standard wheeling tariffs; monthly MW dashboard.  
**Who:** DMRE, NERSA, PTSO  
**Cost:** Neutral (private capex)  
**Impact:** Add 2-3GW capacity in 18 months

### 2. Critical Skills Visa Lane (0-6 months)
**What:** 30-day processing for critical skills; digital queue; employer accreditation.  
**Who:** DHA, DTIC  
**Cost:** Low  
**Impact:** Unblock 50,000+ skilled worker applications

### 3. Conditional Grant Enforcement (0-12 months)
**What:** Withhold/redirect MIG/INEP on underspend; publish S71/S72 dashboards.  
**Who:** Treasury, COGTA  
**Cost:** Savings  
**Impact:** R50bn+ in infrastructure actually delivered

## The Political Economy Problem

Why hasn't this happened? 

The recommendations exist. The evidence is clear. The ROI is compelling. But reform threatens interests:

- **Procurement reform** threatens rent-seekers embedded in state supply chains
- **Consequence management** threatens officials who've benefited from impunity
- **Vacancy filling** threatens unions protecting incumbents
- **Competition enforcement** threatens oligopolies in retail, telecom, logistics

The BRRR data doesn't show a knowledge gap. It shows a will gap.

### Appendix: Methodology

1. **Data Collection:** Downloaded 50 BRRR reports from Parliamentary Monitoring Group archives
2. **Extraction:** Used PDF parsing to extract 5,256 individual recommendations
3. **Categorization:** NLP-based classification into sectors, themes, urgency levels
4. **Scoring:** Actionability scoring based on directive vs passive language
5. **Enrichment:** Added economic context (Stats SA, SARB), load-shedding data (CSIR), international benchmarks
6. **Validation:** Cross-referenced with AG reports, Treasury documents, Operation Vulindlela tracking

All code and data are open source. Reproduce, critique, improve.
